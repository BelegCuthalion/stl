($N'$) As the last rule of $\mathbf{D_0}$, $N'$ forces $A = \nabla B$ for some $B$. Then $\mathbf{D_0}$ proves $\nabla \Gamma \Rightarrow \nabla B$ and has a subproof $\mathbf{D_0}'$ of $\Gamma \Rightarrow B$.
\begin{prooftree}
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_0}'$}
	\UIC{$\Gamma \Rightarrow B$}
	\RightLabel{$N'$}
	\UIC{$\nabla \Gamma \Rightarrow \nabla B$}
	
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_1}$}
	\UIC{$\Sigma , (\nabla^{l+1} B)^n \Rightarrow \Delta$}
	
	\dashedLine \RightLabel{$MC$}
	\BIC{$\nabla^{l+1} \Gamma , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$}
\end{prooftree}
Induction hypothesis on $\mathbf{D_0}'$ and $\mathbf{D_1}$ gives exactly what we want.
\begin{prooftree}
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_0}'$}
	\UIC{$\Gamma \Rightarrow B$}
	
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_1}$}
	\UIC{$\Sigma , (\nabla^{l+1} B)^n \Rightarrow \Delta$}
	
	\RightLabel{IH}
	\BIC{$\nabla^{l+1} \Gamma , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$}
\end{prooftree}
