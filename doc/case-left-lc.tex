$\mathbf{D_0}$ proves $\Gamma , B \Rightarrow A$ and has a subproof $\mathbf{D_0}'$ of $\Gamma , B , B \Rightarrow A$.
\begin{prooftree}
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_0}'$}
	\UIC{$\Gamma , B , B \Rightarrow A$}
	\RightLabel{$Lc$}
	\UIC{$\Gamma , B \Rightarrow A$}
	
	
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_1}$}
	\UIC{$\Sigma , (\nabla^l A)^n \Rightarrow \Delta$}
	
	\dashedLine\RightLabel{$MC$}
	\BIC{$\nabla^l \Gamma , \nabla^l B , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$}
\end{prooftree}
From the induction hypothesis for $\mathbf{D_0}'$ and $\mathbf{D_1}$, we have a proof of $\nabla^l \Gamma , \nabla^l B , \nabla^l B , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$ with rank lower than that of $A$. By applying $Lc$ we have $\nabla^l \Gamma , \nabla^l B , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$, without increasing the cut-rank.
\begin{prooftree}
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_0}'$}
	\UIC{$\Gamma , B , B \Rightarrow A$}
	
	\noLine
	\AXC{$\mathbf{D_1}$}
	\UIC{$\Sigma , (\nabla^l A)^n \Rightarrow \Delta$}
	
	\RightLabel{IH}
	\BIC{$\nabla^l \Gamma , \nabla^l B , \nabla^l B , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$}
	
	\RightLabel{$Lc$}
	\UIC{$\nabla^l \Gamma , \nabla^l B , \Sigma \Rightarrow \Delta$}
\end{prooftree}
